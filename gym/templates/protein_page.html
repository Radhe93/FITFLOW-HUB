Protein Intake Guide

Understand how much protein you need and how to get it!

Why Protein is Important

Protein is essential for muscle repair, immune function, and overall body health. Learn how to make sure you are getting enough. Protein is an essential macronutrient that plays a vital role in nearly every cell and function in the body. It is the building block of muscles, tissues, and organs, helping with growth, repair, and maintenance.

Consuming enough protein is particularly important for muscle recovery after exercise, as it helps to rebuild and strengthen muscle fibers. Protein also supports a healthy immune system, promotes proper enzyme and hormone production, and assists in the transportation of nutrients throughout the body.

Additionally, it plays a key role in maintaining healthy skin, hair, and nails Without adequate protein intake, the body may experience fatigue, muscle weakness, and an increased risk of illness. Therefore, ensuring you get the right amount of protein is essential for overall health and well-being.

How Much Protein Do You Need?

Generally, the recommended daily intake of protein is 0.8 grams per kilogram of body weight. However, athletes and people with higher activity levels may need more.
